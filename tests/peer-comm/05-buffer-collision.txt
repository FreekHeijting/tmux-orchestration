F5 empirical: paste-buffer name collision
Run: 2026-05-10 16:39 UTC
Method: tmux-only (no claude needed; pure paste-buffer mechanics)

Step 1: sender X loads "shared-name" with PAYLOAD-X
Step 2: sender Y loads "shared-name" with PAYLOAD-Y (overwrite)
Step 3: sender X performs paste-buffer -b shared-name into receiver pane

Result (capture-pane of receiver):
freek@host:~$ PAYLOAD-Y-from-sender-Y
PAYLOAD-Y-from-sender-Y: opdracht niet gevonden

Conclusion:
- tmux load-buffer -b NAME silently overwrites any existing buffer of that name.
- Last-loader wins. X's content is gone the instant Y calls load-buffer.
- X's subsequent paste-buffer injects Y's content.
- No warning, no error, silent data corruption.

Mitigation (mandatory):
- Buffer names MUST include sender identity + nanosecond timestamp:
    BUF="peer-${TMO_SESSION}-$(date +%s%N)"
- Two senders cannot collide because (a) different TMO_SESSION values, and
  (b) different nanosecond stamps within one sender.
- ALWAYS delete-buffer after paste-buffer to avoid leaking buffer slots.
